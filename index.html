01 — About
Evelyn S. Goroza Medford, 2025

A researcher-designer working at the intersection of trust, learning, and human behavior, across regulated industries, academic research, and product design.

I recently completed my M.S. in Human Factors Engineering at Tufts, where my thesis investigated how cultural values and personality shape trust calibration in adaptable automation systems. The work led to Use-in-Range, a novel metric I adapted from clinical Time-in-Range to quantify calibrated, misused, and disused reliance on collaborative AI.

Before Tufts, I spent almost two years as a Clinical Research Coordinator at Joslin Diabetes Center, a Harvard Medical School affiliate, where I directed usability and safety testing for Class III medical devices supporting successful FDA 510(k) clearances. I've also worked in global marketing research at Sanofi, delivered BLS-certified critical care in the CVICU during the first year of the pandemic, and run a freelance analog photography practice featured in LensCulture.

The through-line is the same question from different angles: what does it take for a person and a system to work well together? Whether the system is an AI collaborator, a LEGO robot coding app for autistic middle schoolers, or a drowsy-driving detector, the design job is to meet people where they are.

02 — Thesis

Quantifying Calibration

An empirical investigation of how cultural values and dispositional traits shape trust, reliance, and calibrated use of collaborative automation. Supported by surveys, a custom simulation game (Calibratio), and structural path modeling across N = 189 participants.
